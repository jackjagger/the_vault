A type of business that sells products produced by [[manufacturing business |manufacturing businesses]] in the form of inventory. Their primary goal is to sell their products onto consumers who are interested in their business niche. Typical [[transactions]] for retail businesses include:

- Sourcing products produced by manufacturing companies
- Costs of having these products shipped to the business retail location
- Staff expenses
- Customer exchanges via Point of Service machine (buying and returning products)
- Overhead expenses (rent, maintenance, fees)

Sometimes there is overlap between both retail and manufacturing businesses such as car brands who manufacture cars in one country and sell them across the world in a retail space.

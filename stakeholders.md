Someone who has a financial interest in the company and looks to see the economic viability that company has through [[financial accounting]] practices.

Stakeholders encompass a number of individuals and organisations such as but not limited to stockholders, creditors and governmental regulation agencies.

### Stockholders
Individuals or companies that buy stock (a.k.a shares) in a company and provide financial support to make a profit from the business. Stockholders make unbiased decisions based on accounting statements that reflect whether their stocks have increased in valve and whether the corporate stability of company remains so that they can continue to make more profits.

### Creditors
Businesses who provide credit in the form of extended payment terms and have a quick 30 to 45 day turnaround on when that credit needs to be paid back.

### Lenders
Businesses such as banks and regulated financial institutions who provide large sums of credit to businesses that are paid back over years and accrue interest.

#### Risk of Borrowing Money from Creditors and Lenders
Since [[financial accounting]] and [[managerial accounting]] operate on a historical basis, an established accounting record are more likely to see the credit they provide to the business paid back in full. However, there is always a risk that businesses even with sound financial history may not pay back their loans in full.

### Customers
Customers provide financial interest in the most direct way possible; by purchasing products from the business directly. Their involvement is the most important part of any successful business and allows for businesses to expand into other avenues and locations both physically and digitally. Customers are a key part of any type of business including [[retail business]], [[service business]] and [[manufacturing business|manufacturing businesses]].
